For My Marrakesh

A Letter from the Heart

Marrakesh, I don't even know where to start, because loving you feels bigger than words. I just know that my heart always finds its way back to you. I love you in a way that feels calm and wild at the same time. Loving you feels natural, like breathing, like something my soul already knew how to do before I even understood it. You're not just someone I love, you're someone my heart chose without asking for reasons.

You are so beautiful, and I don't mean that in a simple way. Your beauty isn't only in how you look, it's in how you exist. It's in your smile, in the way you talk, in the way you feel things deeply. Sometimes I look at you and I genuinely think you don't even realize how beautiful you are, and that makes you even more beautiful. You shine without trying, and that's rare.

Your soul is something special, something soft and pure, like an angel placed gently into this world. There's kindness in you that feels real, not forced. There's warmth in your heart that makes people feel safe. The way you feel, the way you care, the way you love — it's all so honest. Being loved by you feels like being protected, like being understood without having to explain everything.

Your love supports me more than you know. When I'm tired, when I'm lost, when I feel weak, your feelings lift me up. Even when we're not together physically, your presence stays with me. You give me strength just by being you. Your love makes me believe in myself more, and that's something I'll always be grateful for.

I hate the distance between us. I hate everything that keeps us apart, every reason, every circumstance, every moment that says "not now." The distance hurts, and sometimes it feels unfair. But even with that pain, my love for you doesn't change. If loving you means suffering sometimes, I'll accept it. I'll try my entire life to be with you, because you're worth every struggle, every wait, every hard moment.

I'm so happy and proud that we've completed a year together. A whole year of love, memories, laughs, tears, understanding, misunderstandings, problems, and growth. We didn't only share good times, we shared real times. We passed troubles together, and that means more to me than perfection ever could. What we have is real, and that's why I believe we'll pass the big problem we're facing now too — together, just like we always have.
